Self-Introduction
The first 90 seconds set the tone for everything that follows
Answer strategy
Structure your answer in three layers: who you are, what you bring that is unique, and why you are here for this specific role. Keep it under 90 seconds. In a German academic hospital interview this is expected to be professional and structured — not casual. Do not read your CV chronologically. Emphasise the triple combination that makes you rare: clinical domain knowledge, health IT at scale, and technical execution capability.
Your prepared answer (adapt as needed)
"I am a licensed pharmacist with a professional permit under §11 BApO in Bavaria and an M.Sc. in Digital Health from DIT, with a grade of 1.6. My thesis was clinically supervised at LMU Klinikum Munich by Prof. Dr. Klaus Parhofer — it received an excellent rating and a clinical utility score of 8.7 out of 10.

Before my Master's, I spent two years as a Senior Digital Transformation Officer at the Egyptian Health Authority — where I led the national EHR rollout across hospital networks, deploying HL7-FHIR R4, ICD-10, SNOMED CT, and DRG standards in production. I also developed three live digital health systems that are currently in use or under active implementation.

What brings me here specifically is the Treuhandstelle. Consent management and pseudonymisation are not abstract concepts for me — they sit exactly at the intersection of patient rights, GDPR compliance, and health data infrastructure, which is precisely where I have been working. I see this role as the natural next step where I can contribute leadership as well as technical depth."
⚠️ Do NOT say "full approbation" — you hold a professional permit §11 BApO (supervised). Do NOT say your German is C1 — it is B2 plus the pharmaceutical specialist language exam (BLAK 2025).

Leadership & Team
This is a Leitung role — they will probe your leadership thinking hard
Answer strategy
In a German academic hospital context, leadership is expected to be structured, transparent, and professionally grounded. Avoid vague terms like "servant leader" without substance. Be specific about your approach and anchor it in real experience — your role as Deputy Administrative Director at the chest hospital and your national rollout with 500+ trained professionals are genuine leadership evidence.
Your prepared answer
"I lead by making expectations clear and giving people the context to work independently. I believe a team in a compliance-sensitive environment like a Treuhandstelle needs to understand not just what to do, but why — because when unusual situations arise, they need to make the right decision without waiting for me to approve every step.

In practical terms: I set clear SOPs, I hold regular structured check-ins, I give direct feedback, and I protect the team's autonomy on routine decisions while keeping accountability central for anything involving data protection risk. I have led large multi-site training programmes and coordinated between clinical, IT, and administrative stakeholders at national level — that gave me a realistic view of how much of leadership is actually communication and not just authority."

For a Treuhandstelle Leitung role, the most relevant strengths are: technical depth combined with the ability to explain it simply, structured process thinking, and credibility with both clinical and IT stakeholders.

Questions to Ask the Interviewers
Asking the right questions is half the interview — it signals depth and genuine interest
🔧
"Are gICS and gPAS already deployed and in production here, or is this role expected to lead their initial implementation?"
Shows you know what the tools are and understand the difference between running an existing system and setting one up from scratch — two very different jobs.
👥
"How many people are currently in the THS team, and what is the balance of technical versus administrative roles?"
Legitimate Leitung question — you need to understand what you are inheriting before you can plan.
🔗
"What does the working relationship with the DIZ look like in practice — shared infrastructure, shared data releases, or separate parallel processes?"
Shows you understand the MII architecture and want to understand how Frankfurt specifically has implemented the THS-DIZ boundary.
📋
"What does success look like in the first twelve months — what would you want this role to have achieved?"
Classic strong closer. Gets them talking about their priorities and helps you understand whether your 90-day plan matches their expectations.
🌐
"How actively does the Frankfurt THS participate in MII, MIRACUM, and NUM working groups — is that something this Leitung role is expected to drive?"
Shows national ecosystem awareness and asks them to be explicit about the scope of the external-facing element of the role.
📄
"The posting mentions developing anonymisation and pseudonymisation guidelines — is there an existing policy framework, or is this largely to be built?"
Directly references the job posting and shows you have read it carefully. Also tells you how much greenfield work is involved.
